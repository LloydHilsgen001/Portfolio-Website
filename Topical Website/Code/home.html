Nuclear Power

Driving innovation and the key to our future power needs

With the now ever present fear of climate change, nuclear power is safe, easily obtainable, and not the danger it once posed.

Efficient

One ton of thorium produces as much energy as 200 tons of Uranium, and 3,500,000 tons of coal.

A thorium reactor's plutonium production rate would be less than 2 percent of that of a standard reactor

and the plutonium's isotopic content would make it unsuitable for a nuclear detonation.

Sustainable

Thorium is one of the most common actinides, and is often found along side rare earth metals like gold and neodymium.

The current reserves of thorium in the US alone could power it for the next 1,000 years without any carbon emissions.

Current Thorium reserves could produce 15x the amount of energy that our coal, natural gas, and oil reserves combined will produce.

Because thorium reactors are practically meltdown proof, scaling energy needs can be met by them without risk of ecological damage
